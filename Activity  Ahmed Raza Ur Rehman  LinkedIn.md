    73 out of 100. Alhamdulillah.  
      
    Not because 73 is good. But because I walked out thinking I barely got 15, only the ones I was completely sure about.  
      
    I did not study. Did not prepare. Went in with the kind of confidence that comes from never exposing myself to the market. Thought it would be easy. Blank confidence. I sat there picking answers on instinct and gut feeling, walked out with a bitter taste of defeat and embarrassment, and genuinely believed I had failed. The test humbled me in a way I needed. That is on me and I own that.  
      
    Then I saw the percentile.  
      
    98.5th. 119 universities. Over 40,700 final-year CS and IT students. The best we are about to send into the industry.  
      
    These were not trick questions. Not advanced research topics. Absolute fundamentals. Things a 7th or 8th semester student should be scoring close to 100 on, not because the bar is high but because these are the bare minimum the industry expects.  
      
    And going in completely unprepared put me above 98.5% of them.  
      
    That is not something to feel good about. That is something to sit with and think seriously about.  
      
    Every generation used to outdo the one before it. Back when I was in 9th grade, scoring 98% still left you around the 70th percentile nationally. That was the standard we were chasing. What happened to that hunger?  
      
    This is not just a CS and IT problem. It is a generational one. IT was just affected most by it.  
      
    Somewhere along the way we got comfortable with the illusion of knowing without actually knowing. We stopped sitting with hard things long enough to understand them. Social media, short videos, endless scrolling took the hours that used to go into building real depth. And now AI is doing the thinking on top of that. Developed countries are already moving to restrict social media for under 18s. There is a reason.  
      
    This is not on our teachers. Not on our institutions. My teachers were the reason I was able to score even that 73, even when I was not attentive or hardworking. The disgrace is solely on me.  
      
    I am genuinely grateful to [Higher Education Commission](https://www.linkedin.com/company/higher-education-commission/) for creating this benchmark. We needed to see where we stand. But I hope it does not stop at a number. I hope it leads to something structural, something that raises the floor and pushes us as graduates, as institutions, as a community to move with more intention toward building a good future for this country.  
      
    The crisis is real. But so is the ability to turn it around, with the very same things that caused it, if we use them right this time.  
      
    Thank you, [Institute of Space Technology Kicsit Campus](https://www.linkedin.com/company/institute-of-space-technology-kicsit-campus/) ,[Higher Education Commission](https://www.linkedin.com/company/higher-education-commission/), P@SHA,[National Computing Education Accreditation Council - NCEAC](https://www.linkedin.com/company/nceac/)
